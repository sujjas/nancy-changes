The Full Story

From Kampala's airwaves to the world stage

Nancy Kacungira is a lead presenter and foreign correspondent for BBC News, anchoring The World Today. With over fifteen years across Uganda, Kenya, Tanzania, and the UK, she has built a career at the intersection of storytelling, influence, and strategic communication.

Her journey began at Power FM in Kampala while studying at Makerere University — graduating with first-class honours. She moved to NTV Uganda, then Kenya's KTN, before winning the inaugural BBC Komla Dumor Award in 2015.

At the BBC, she has presented Focus on Africa, World Business Report, In Business Africa, and moderated at the World Economic Forum, FT Africa Summit, and IMF/World Bank Spring Meetings.

She holds an MA in International Communications from the University of Leeds (distinction) and co-founded Blu Flamingo, a digital agency across four African markets.
